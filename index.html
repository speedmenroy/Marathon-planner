Nutrition Guide

Vegetarian runner nutrition

🏃 Base Building (Weeks 1–4)

Build aerobic foundation

▼
Calories: +200–300 above maintenance
Carbs: 5–6g/kg/day
Protein: 1.4–1.6g/kg/day
Fat: 25–30% total calories
Key foods:
  • Oats, sweet potato, brown rice
  • Lentils, chickpeas, tofu
  • Leafy greens, berries
  • Nuts, seeds, avocado

Timing: Eat complex carbs 2–3hrs before runs. Post-run: protein + carbs within 30min.

⚡ Build Phase (Weeks 5–8)

Increase intensity and volume

▼
Calories: +400–500 above maintenance
Carbs: 6–8g/kg/day
Protein: 1.6–1.8g/kg/day
Fat: 20–25% total calories
Key foods:
  • Quinoa, pasta, rice cakes
  • Tempeh, edamame, Greek yoghurt
  • Beetroot, tart cherry juice
  • Electrolyte-rich foods

Timing: Pre-long run carb loading starts night before. Mid-run fuelling every 45min for runs >90min.

🎯 Peak / Race Prep (Weeks 9–10)

Maximum training load

▼
Calories: +500–700 above maintenance
Carbs: 8–10g/kg/day
Protein: 1.8–2.0g/kg/day
Fat: 20% total calories
Key foods:
  • High-GI carbs on hard days
  • Iron-rich foods daily
  • Anti-inflammatory foods
  • Hydration focus: 35–40ml/kg/day

Timing: Practice race-day nutrition in long runs. Identify your gel/fuel strategy.

🏁 Taper & Recovery (Weeks 11–12)

Carb load, rest, race

▼
Calories: Maintain (reduce slightly)
Carbs: 8–10g/kg days 2–3 pre-race
Protein: 1.6g/kg/day
Fat: Low during carb load
Key foods:
  • White rice, pasta, bread (carb load)
  • Familiar, low-fibre foods
  • Avoid new foods race week
  • Extra sodium day before

Race morning: 2–3hrs before: 1–2g/kg carbs (toast, banana, rice). Coffee 60min before if habitual.

🥜 Protein

96g / day

~1.6g/kg for 60kg runner

  • Tempeh 100g = 19g protein
  • Lentils 1 cup cooked = 18g
  • Tofu 100g = 17g protein
  • Greek yoghurt 200g = 17g
  • Edamame 1 cup = 17g protein
  • Chickpeas 1 cup = 15g protein
  • Quinoa 1 cup = 8g protein

🩸 Iron

23mg / day

Higher than meat-eaters (1.8× absorption factor)

  • Pumpkin seeds 30g = 4.2mg
  • Lentils 1 cup = 6.6mg
  • Chickpeas 1 cup = 4.7mg
  • Spinach 1 cup cooked = 3.5mg
  • Tofu 100g = 3.0mg
  • Quinoa 1 cup = 2.8mg
  • Always pair with Vitamin C

💊 Vitamin B12

2.4mcg / day

Critical for nerve function and red blood cells

  • Fortified nutritional yeast 2tbsp = 4mcg
  • Fortified plant milk 1 cup = 1.2mcg
  • Eggs 2 large = 1.0mcg
  • Dairy yoghurt 200g = 1.0mcg
  • Supplement 500–1000mcg recommended

🦴 Calcium

1000mg / day

Bone health, muscle contraction

  • Fortified plant milk 1 cup = 300mg
  • Tofu (calcium-set) 100g = 350mg
  • Kale 1 cup cooked = 180mg
  • Bok choy 1 cup = 160mg
  • Almonds 30g = 75mg
  • Separate calcium and iron meals by 2hrs

🐟 Omega-3

1.6g ALA / day

Anti-inflammatory, joint health

  • Flaxseeds 1 tbsp = 2.3g ALA
  • Chia seeds 1 tbsp = 1.7g ALA
  • Walnuts 30g = 2.5g ALA
  • Hemp seeds 3 tbsp = 1.0g ALA
  • Algae-based DHA/EPA supplement recommended

☀️ Vitamin D

800 IU / day

Bone health, immune function, mood

  • Sunlight 15–20min daily (best source)
  • Fortified plant milk 1 cup = 100 IU
  • Fortified cereal 1 serving = 40–80 IU
  • Mushrooms UV-exposed 100g = 400 IU
  • Supplement D3 (vegan) 1000–2000 IU recommended

  • Lentil soup with brown rice

Race Day Morning

  • Evening: anti-inflammatory (turmeric milk)

💡 Testing Recommendation
Get blood tests every 3–6 months: Full Blood Count, Serum Ferritin (iron stores), B12, Vitamin D, and Zinc. Don't supplement without testing first — over-supplementation of iron is harmful.

Heme vs Non-Heme Iron

As a vegetarian, you consume non-heme iron only. This has 2–20% absorption vs 15–35% for heme iron from meat.

The good news:

Your body upregulates absorption when stores are low. With correct meal timing and Vitamin C pairing, you can significantly boost non-heme absorption to 8–12%.

Why Runners Need More

  • Foot-strike haemolysis destroys red blood cells
  • GI blood losses during long runs
  • Sweat iron losses (~0.3mg/L sweat)
  • Inflammation reduces iron absorption
  • Hepcidin spike post-exercise blocks absorption for 3–6hrs

Best time to take iron: Morning, fasted, before exercise

Race Day Alerts

⛰️ Elevation & Course Profile
Study the elevation profile before race day. Kanazawa Marathon has rolling hills — start conservatively on uphills (shorten stride, keep effort constant). Save energy for downhills by leaning slightly forward. Don't race the uphills.
🏃 Pacing Strategy
Start 10–15 sec/km slower than target pace for first 10km. Negative split is the goal — run second half slightly faster. Bank time mentally, not physically. Resist running with the crowd.
🍌 Race Nutrition Timing
First gel at 45min, then every 30–45min. Never try new products on race day. Drink at every aid station even if not thirsty (small sips). Electrolytes are critical in humidity.
🌡️ Weather Preparation
Kanazawa in October: avg 15–20°C, possible rain. Adjust pace for heat — add ~20 sec/km per 5°C above 15°C. Dress in moisture-wicking layers. Vaseline for chafe prevention.
👟 Gear Checklist
Race-day checklist: Race bib + safety pins, timing chip, shoes (run in them 3+ times before), socks (tested), GPS watch charged, nutrition gels x6, electrolyte tabs, anti-chafe balm, throwaway warm layer for start.
🧠 Mental Checkpoints
Break marathon into thirds: 0–14km (hold back), 14–28km (race your pace), 28–42km (race your courage). When it gets hard at 30–35km, focus on 1km at a time. Your training got you here — trust it.
